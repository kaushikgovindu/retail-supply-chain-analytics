### 1.1 Business Context
Staples Canada operates 300+ stores and multiple fulfillment centers (FCs) across Ontario and Canada, serving both retail and B2B customers with office supplies, technology, and furniture. The Supply Chain Analytics team faces pressure to improve replenishment efficiency, reduce stockouts, and optimize transportation costs between FCs and stores. Recent challenges include inconsistent lead times from FCs, stockouts of high-demand SKUs at key stores, and rising transportation costs per unit delivered. Leadership needs actionable insights and KPI dashboards to guide data-driven decisions for FC Operations, Replenishment, and Transportation teams.

### 1.2 Core Analytical Questions
- Which stores and product categories experience the highest stockout frequency and backorder rates?
- How do replenishment lead times vary by Fulfillment Center and store region (Ontario vs. other provinces)?
- What is the on-time delivery rate for FC-to-store shipments by carrier and route?
- Which FC-store lanes have the highest transportation cost per unit and lowest fill rates?
- How does inventory turnover correlate with stockouts and holding costs across product categories?
- Where should reorder points and shipment frequency be adjusted to balance service levels and costs?

### 1.3 Core Supply Chain KPIs Tracked
- Fill Rate (%) – % of ordered units successfully shipped from FC to store
- Stockout Rate (%) – % of store orders unable to fulfill due to inventory shortage  
- On-Time Delivery (%) – % of shipments arriving at store within committed window
- Inventory Turnover – How many times inventory is sold/replenished annually
- Replenishment Lead Time (Days) – Average days from order to store receipt
- Transportation Cost per Unit – Total shipping cost ÷ units delivered
- Perfect Order Rate (%) – % of orders delivered complete, on-time, damage-free
- Days Inventory on Hand – Average days supply currently in store/FC
